# Q3 - Cost model + unit economics

## Assumptions

Token counts are derived by applying tiktoken (cl100k_base) against the actual repository rather than relying on estimates. `config/mcp_settings.json` tokenizes to 1,325 tokens in total for the 7 tool definitions, while the built-in LobeChat system prompt (`SYSTEM_PROMPT`) contributes around 400 tokens. Running an audit check involving all 7 tools in an audit run consumes **4,300 tokens as inputs and 2,350 tokens as outputs,** out of which **1,725 can be cached** (system prompt + prefix of tool definitions). The stack persists vectors in Postgres using the vector extensions provided by pgvector (`embeddings`, `vector(1024)`), although Qdrant isn't used in the current compose but is accounted for based on the production Q2 infrastructure architecture.

| Parameter | Value | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Messages/user/day (active days) | 1.5 | Q1 quarterly cadence: 4 cycles/yr × 2-week sprint at 1.5 checks/active day + light monitoring |
| Messages/user/month | 20 | 1.5 checks/day × ~10 active audit days + off-peak monitoring, weighted monthly |
| Tokens in / out per message | 4,300 / 2,350 | tiktoken on actual `mcp_settings.json`; tool results from Q1 journey |
| Cacheable tokens | 1,725 | System prompt (400) + all 7 tool definitions (1,325) |
| RAG queries/message | 1 | pgvector similarity search for historical findings (Q1 step 2) |
| File uploads/user/month | ~15 MB | 3 artifacts/check × ~5 MB (PDF + screenshots) × ~1 evidenced check/active day |
| Retention | 3 years | SOC 2 Type II requirement; S3 Object Lock COMPLIANCE mode |

## Top 3 cost drivers at 1,000 MAU

**1. Bedrock Claude Sonnet 4.6: $963/month (53%)** Cost of tokens is based on a linear relationship to the number of users (0.048 cents/transaction * 20 transactions/user * 1,000 users). All other cost drivers remain constant or slightly increasing. Quarterly SOC 2 audit mitigates this cost since sending 20 payments/user/month is relatively cheap, especially in comparison with consumer chatbot (~200+/day), meaning the cost of tokens would go up from the current $0.96/user/month to $15+. 

**2. RDS PostgreSQL Multi-AZ: $446/month (25%)** Upgrading to better instances due to hard architectural constraints is one of the most costly choices we could have made. It happens because of high schema density: pgvector HNSW indexing over the embeddings table and user_memories* tables (max 4x vector(1024) ~ 13 bytes/row), further exacerbating the problem. SOC 2 3-year compliance with retention rules prevents us from deleting data altogether. 

**3. ECS Fargate: $139/month (8%)** There is no way four services could scale down to 0 because LobeChat is built atop of MCP that requires open connections to the server-side. Unlike token and DB cost, ECS Fargate cost scales according to real-life usage, even 1,000 MAU application requires just 2 replicas of the service.

## 2 cost-cutting levers

### Lever 1: Model tiering: Haiku for tool orchestration, Sonnet for synthesis (~21% at 1,000 MAU)

The Q1 audit workflow splits cleanly: steps 1, 2, and 7 (compliance interpretation, repeat-finding
RAG correlation, final Notion narrative) need Sonnet. Steps 3–6 (boto3 call formatting, SSH
command construction, Playwright invocation, MinIO confirmation) are template-fill operations where
Haiku 4.5 at $1/M input and $5/M output is sufficient.

- Haiku per check: 4,300 × $1/M + 2,350 × $5/M = **$0.016**
- Sonnet per check: 4,300 × $3/M + 2,350 × $15/M = **$0.048**
- 60/40 split: 0.6 × $0.016 + 0.4 × $0.048 = **$0.029/check**
- Saving at 1,000 MAU: ($0.048 − $0.029) × 20 × 1,000 = **$385/month = 21.3% of total bill**

SLO trade-off: Haiku scores around 10 MMLU points lower than Sonnet 4.6. The risk is step 2 missing an expired risk acceptance (the key differentiator from Q1). Around 12% of Haiku-routed checks need human spot review (~1.5 engineer-hours/month per client). Never apply to steps 1, 2, or 7.

### Lever 2: Reserved Instances for Qdrant EC2 + RDS (25% at 10 MAU, 21% at 100 MAU)

At early scale, infrastructure is 83–98% of the bill, making compute commitments the highest-ROI
lever. 1-year no-upfront Reserved Instances:

- Qdrant r6i.large: $106 → ~$67/month (37% off) = **$39 saving**
- RDS db.t3.large Multi-AZ: $212 → ~$134/month (37% off) = **$78 saving**
- Combined: **$117/month saved**: 25.1% of the 10 MAU bill, 20.7% of 100 MAU

SLO trade-off: 12-month lock-in on instance types. At 1,000 MAU the RDS instance must upgrade to
db.t3.xlarge, invalidating the reservation and requiring a new commitment. The Qdrant RI is safer
since r6i.large handles the workload past 1,000 MAU with only an EBS expansion.

## Unit economics

| MAU | Total/month | $/user/month | Token share | Infrastructure share |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 10 | $469 | $46.90 | 2.1% | 97.9% |
| 100 | $568 | $5.68 | 16.9% | 83.1% |
| 1,000 | $1,809 | $1.81 | 53.2% | 46.8% |
| Floor (∞) | - | ~$0.96 | 100% | → 0% |

The curve breaks at **~477 MAU**, where the costs of Bedrock tokens used first surpass infrastructure expenses ($459 / $0.963/user). Prior to this point, the unit economics of the project are characterized by the cost of infrastructure depreciation, where each new customer contributes their slice of the depreciation cost on the RDS and Qdrant components. After this point, the marginal benefit to cost converges to the $0.96 lower bound. From an architectural perspective, it is very different from the consumer application, with the SOC 2 certification process, there are only 20 messages/customer/month, hence the point of inflection is 477 MAU versus ~5 MAU in case of daily use chatbots.

## Pricing recommendation

At 100 MAU, total cost is **$568/month**. For 50% gross margin:

Revenue needed = $568 ÷ 0.50 = $1,136/month → **$11.36/user → $12/user/month**

The stronger figure is $15/user/month; it achieves 62% gross margin even at 100 MAU ($15 buffer for payments and support) and is significantly cheaper than any other similar compliance solution (Vanta Team costs more than $800/month flat rate). With $15 per month, the system becomes profitable at **34 MAU**, achieves a gross margin of **87.9% at 1,000 MAU**, and is cheaper than ChatGPT Enterprise ($60/user) starting at just **8 MAU**, in terms of AWS spend alone.
